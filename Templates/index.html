Cybercrime in the Indian Banking Sector: Challenges and Prospects

PROJECT INTRODUCTION

The rapid digitization of India's banking sector has led to significant advancements in financial services but has also increased vulnerabilities to cybercrime. Cyber threats such as phishing, ransomware attacks, identity theft, and banking fraud have escalated, posing challenges to financial institutions, regulators, and customers. The evolving nature of cyber threats demands robust cybersecurity frameworks, continuous monitoring, and proactive risk management strategies. This paper explores the key challenges faced by the Indian banking sector, including regulatory gaps, technological weaknesses, and human factors contributing to cybercrime. It also examines the prospects of enhancing cybersecurity through advanced technologies like artificial intelligence (AI), blockchain, and biometric authentication. Strengthening cybersecurity awareness, enforcing stricter regulations, and fostering public-private collaboration are critical to mitigating risks and ensuring a secure banking ecosystem. The study emphasizes the need for a dynamic, multi-layered approach to counter cyber threats and build a resilient financial infrastructure in India.
